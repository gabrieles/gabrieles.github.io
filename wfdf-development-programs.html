Development projects

Development projects are eligible for WFDF funding, and you may may find support from our partners, and from the community, too!

Development projects is a term used to describe a broad range of projects, including the introduction of Flying Disc sports into new countries, the expansion of Flying Disc sports activities in developing countries, gender inclusion projects, programs for spirit of the game education, youth programs, coaches training, anti-doping education, and other areas.

To apply for support for a development project you need to complete the “Ask” form on this site. The WFDF Sport for Development Commission will then review your submission and contact you to discuss the proposal. If accepted, we will work with you to publish a page to showcase your project, and will be included in our communications. We want to support you to find visibility and support from our community, but also to share the good ideas from your proposal to inspire future applications.

How to apply for a WFDF Grant

WFDF grants can be for up to US$1,500, and may go to fund activities for up to 15 months from the date of the award. The program is not limited to projects sponsored by WFDF member countries but explicitly calls for initiatives or projects by individuals or groups, particularly in new countries. The main criteria for selection are the project's sustainability and long lasting impact in the affected area. If you receive a grant from the WFDF, you will be required to submit a final report on your activities and their outcome.

To apply for a WFDF grant you need to complete a form (you can download it here) and then upload it via the “Ask” form on this site.

How to receive support from our partners

Once your proposal has been accepted, you will be asked to submit a short project description, and any images/video that you may have to create your project page on this site. Once this is ready, we will publish the page, and we will advertise it via our channels.

This will include sending your proposal to the relevant WFDF partners, and advertising how the community may support you (eg, if you have a fundraising initiative we can give out the links)
